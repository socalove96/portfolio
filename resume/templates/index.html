My Journey

I started purchasing stocks and teaching myself how to build websites while studying business in college. Upon graduation, I held a summer internship at Ford Motor Company, successfully learned to day trade (profitably), and developed responsive, data-driven websites for small businesses using the Python web development framework called Django.


My interests in economics and finance were harnessed during my studies at McMaster University. I spent much of my time embroiled in the works of renowned economists Thomas Piketty, and Erik Brynjolfsson, which provoked entrepreneurial ideas in the areas of computer automation using digital robots at the user interface level instead of the traditional application level. I created a startup with two Co-Founders I met on Linkedin. We concentrated on the distribution of robotic process automation systems for businesses searching to automate repetitive processes.


I worked as an intern Financial Analyst at Jaguar Land Rover where I was tasked with compiling an important document many Senior Associates rejected. I saw this as an opportunity to prove I was ready and willing to make a real effort for the team. I created a P&L assessment using financial models and information obtained from all areas of the organization. I had to speak with representatives from every department to obtain hidden information, I had to teach myself corporate applications on the fly, and I consulted with the GM regularly to provide updates on progress. Altogether, I created an outstanding document that surpassed all expectations and rewarded our organization with additional subsidies.


Lastly, I operated a home renovation business until I graduated from university. I am currently analyzing stock prices and macroeconomic trends using statistical packages in Python until I find an opportunity to work in finance.
